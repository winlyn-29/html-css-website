About Capybaras

The capybara is the world's largest rodent, reaching up to 4.5 feet in length and weighing between 100 to 150 pounds. These gentle creatures are native to South America, where they are commonly found in the wild in places like Venezuela, Colombia, and Brazil. Capybaras are semiaquatic animals that thrive in habitats near rivers, lakes, and wetlands. They are highly social creatures and typically live in large groups of up to 40 individuals.

Despite their large size, capybaras are very calm and non-aggressive. They have webbed feet that help them navigate through water efficiently, and they are also strong swimmers. Capybaras are herbivores and graze on grass and other vegetation found near the water's edge. Their social structure and friendly demeanor make them a favorite among other animals and even humans.

Capybaras communicate with each other through various vocalizations, including barks, whistles, and clicks. Their docile nature makes them a popular animal in zoos and wildlife reserves worldwide. Because of their strong social bonds and placid nature, capybaras are often seen as the "dogs of the rodent world." They are admired for their relaxed and easygoing attitude, even around potential threats.
